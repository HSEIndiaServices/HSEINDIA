ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (EMS) TRAINING

ABOUT THE TRAINING:

Environmental Management Systems (EMS) training focuses on educating individuals about the principles, processes, and implementation of effective EMS to manage an organization's environmental responsibilities. EMS is a systematic approach to integrating environmental considerations into an organization's operations, decision-making, and culture. Here are the typical contents of an EMS training program:

    TRAINING CONTENTS

  • Introduction to Environmental Management Systems.
  • Key Elements of an EMS.
  • Environmental Policy and Commitment.
  • Identifying Environmental Aspects and Impacts.
  • Identification of Aspects and Impacts.
  • Legal and Regulatory Requirements.
  • Setting Objectives and Targets.
  • Environmental Management Programs.
  • Operational Control and Procedures.
  • Training and Competence.
  • Communication and Engagement.
  • Emergency Preparedness and Response.
  • Monitoring and Measurement.
  • Non-Conformance and Corrective Action.
  • Auditing and Management Review.
  • Continual Improvement.
  • Integration with Other Management Systems.
  • Environmental Performance Reporting.
  • Sustainability and Future Trends.
