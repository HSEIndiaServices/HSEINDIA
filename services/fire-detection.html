FIRE DETECTION SYSTEM

ABOUT THE FIRE DETECTION SYSTEM

Fire detection systems are essential safety components in buildings and industrial facilities. They are designed to detect the presence of fire or smoke and provide early warnings to occupants, enabling prompt evacuation and minimizing fire damage.

    FIRE DETECTION SYSTEM TYPES

  • Addressable Systems: In addressable systems, each detector or device has a unique address, allowing for precise identification of the location of an alarm or fault..
  • Conventional Systems: Conventional systems divide a building into zones, and detectors within each zone share a common circuit. While they provide a general location of an alarm, they are less precise than addressable systems..
