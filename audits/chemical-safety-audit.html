CHEMICAL SAFETY AUDIT

ABOUT THE AUDIT:

A chemical safety audit is a systematic assessment of an organization's processes, practices, and systems related to the safe handling, storage, use, and disposal of chemicals. The primary goal of a chemical safety audit is to identify potential hazards, non-compliance with regulations, and areas for improvement to ensure the health and safety of employees, the community, and the environment.
Chemical safety audits are essential to prevent accidents, protect the health of employees and the environment, and ensure that an organization is following best practices in chemical management. These audits help organizations identify weaknesses in their chemical safety programs and take proactive measures to mitigate risks and continuously improve their chemical safety practices.

    AUDIT CONTENTS

  • Regulatory Compliance.
  • Inventory Management.
  • Storage and Handling.
  • Safety Data Sheets (SDS).
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).
  • Training and Awareness.
  • Emergency Preparedness.
  • Waste Management.
  • Process Safety.
  • Documentation and Record-Keeping.
  • Audit Report and Recommendations.
