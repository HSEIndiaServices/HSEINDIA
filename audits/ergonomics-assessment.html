ERGONOMICS ASSESSMENTS

ABOUT THE ASSESSMENTS:

An ergonomics assessment is a systematic evaluation of the design, layout, and organization of workspaces, equipment, tools, and tasks to ensure that they are optimized for the well-being, comfort, and safety of employees. The goal of an ergonomics assessment is to identify potential ergonomic risk factors and make adjustments that promote efficiency, prevent discomfort, and reduce the risk of musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs) and other workplace injuries.
Ergonomics assessments are especially important in workplaces where employees perform repetitive tasks, spend long hours at a computer, or engage in physically demanding activities. Proper ergonomics design can help prevent injuries and enhance overall job satisfaction. Ergonomics assessments help organizations create a healthier and more productive work environment by reducing the risk of workplace injuries, increasing employee comfort, and enhancing job satisfaction.

    ASSESSMENTS CONTENTS

  • Workspace Design.
  • Furniture and Equipment.
  • Task Analysis.
  • Lifting and Manual Handling.
  • Repetitive Motion.
  • Work Breaks and Rotation.
  • Training and Education.
  • Audit Report and Recommendations.
