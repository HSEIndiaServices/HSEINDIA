CONTRACTOR SAFETY AUDIT

ABOUT THE AUDIT:

A contractor safety audit is a thorough assessment of the safety practices, policies, and procedures of contractors working within an organization's premises or on its projects. The purpose of this audit is to ensure that contractors are adhering to safety regulations, implementing effective safety measures, and minimizing risks to themselves and others while performing their work. Contractor safety audits are crucial because contractors often bring their own workforce, equipment, and practices onto a site, which can introduce new safety challenges.
Contractor safety audits are essential to ensure that contractors working on a project or within a facility are following the same safety standards and practices as the organization's own employees. These audits help minimize potential hazards, reduce risks, and maintain a safe working environment for everyone involved.

    AUDIT CONTENTS

  • Contractor Selection and Qualification.
  • Contract Review.
  • Safety Programs and Policies.
  • Hazard Identification.
  • Safety Training.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).
  • Emergency Response and Communication.
  • Safety Records.
  • Site-Specific Safety Rules.
  • Audit Report and Recommendations.
